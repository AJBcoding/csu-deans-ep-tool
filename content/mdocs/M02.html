M02

What the earnings figure measures. The numerator is the median federal-tax-return earnings of program completers four years after completion, restricted to graduates who are not still in school and are working.

The earnings figure compared against the benchmark is the median of a specific population: graduates who completed the program four years prior, who appear in IRS records for the measurement year, who are not enrolled in any institution of higher education, and who have positive earnings.

How it works

  • Population. Graduates who received Title IV funds for enrollment in the program and completed it during the academic year four years before the measurement year.
  • Restriction A — not still in school. Graduates currently enrolled in any institution of higher education are excluded from the numerator.
  • Restriction B — working. Graduates without IRS-reported earnings for the measurement year are excluded.
  • Aggregation. The median is taken over the resulting subset.
  • Suppression. If fewer than 16 IRS reporters appear in the matched cohort, the earnings figure is suppressed by federal privacy rule and the program lands in NOT MEASURED (M12).

The four-year-after horizon is statutorily fixed at OBBBA § 84001(c)(2). Graduates who are continuing their education — for example, a baccalaureate completer who entered a master's program — are excluded from their baccalaureate program's numerator, even if they are also working. This narrows the measured cohort relative to total completers.
