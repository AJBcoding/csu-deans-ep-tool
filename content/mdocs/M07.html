M07

Disclosure obligations under proposed § 668.43(d)(1). First-failure release date triggers a written notice on admissions materials; pre-existing 2023 GE-rule language is the procedural model.

A program's first failure produces an immediate disclosure obligation, separate from and earlier than the eligibility-loss consequence. The institution must surface a federal-warning notice on admissions, recruitment, and student-facing materials for the failing program.

How it works

  • Trigger. Disclosure obligations attach on the first-failure release date — the public release of the federal data showing the program failed for that measurement year.
  • Form. The Department's proposed § 668.43(d)(1) language requires a written notice that the program has failed the earnings-premium test, on admissions materials and recruitment communications, in a Department-prescribed form.
  • Timing. Notice must be in place before the next enrollment cohort accepts admission. The 2023 Gainful Employment final rule's disclosure procedure is the operative procedural model for compliance review.
  • Continuing obligation. The disclosure persists through the second-failure determination. Once eligibility is lost, the disclosure shifts under proposed § 668.604 to a different mandated form.

The disclosure obligation is independent of any appeal. Filing an appeal under § 668.603 does not stay the disclosure requirement; the failing program must publish the warning while the appeal is pending.
