The Department's named populations. The Department's own analysis flags some CIP families as 10–20× more likely to fail; per-CIP elevation factors recomputed against federal data run higher than the prose.

The Department's own regulatory-impact analysis identifies named CIP families that the rule disproportionately affects. The tool surfaces this empirical record alongside per-program verdicts so that named-population programs see the broader pattern in their own context.

How it works

The Department's analysis identifies CIP families with elevated failure risk relative to baseline. The tool reports this in two ways:

  • Named-population flag. Programs in CIP 50.05 (Drama / Theatre Arts and Stagecraft), 50.07 (Fine and Studio Arts), 50.09 (Music), and 51.15 (Mental and Social Health Services) — among others identified by the Department — receive an inline annotation referencing the elevated-risk finding.
  • Recomputed elevation factor. The tool recomputes the per-CIP failure-rate elevation against the most recent federal data the Department uses. For some named CIP families, the recomputed factor runs higher than the Department's prose summary.

This panel does not change the verdict. It surfaces empirical context for programs the Department itself has named as disproportionately exposed. The snapshot-metadata footer reports the federal-data release and the recomputation date so the Dean can see whether the elevation factor reflects the most recent published data.

The named-population finding is descriptive, not procedural. It does not create an appeal pathway or a procedural defense; M09 covers what is and is not appealable.
