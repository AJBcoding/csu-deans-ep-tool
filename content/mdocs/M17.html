M17

Borrower-defense and closed-school discharge timelines under OBBBA §§ 85001–85002. The forgiveness backstop is suspended for newly originated loans; the suspension interacts with the earnings-premium consequence calendar.

OBBBA §§ 85001–85002 suspend the 2022 final rule's borrower-defense (BD) and closed-school-discharge (CSD) provisions for loans originated after the suspension's effective date. A program that loses Direct Loan eligibility under § 84001 produces students with limited federal-loan exposure; but a program that produced borrowers under the prior regime carries the BD/CSD interaction forward.

How it works

  • What § 85001 suspends. The 2022 final rule (87 FR 65904) created procedural pathways for borrower-defense claims based on institutional misconduct or substantial misrepresentation. § 85001 suspends those provisions for newly originated loans; the underlying statutory borrower-defense authority is unaffected.
  • What § 85002 suspends. Closed-school-discharge timelines under the 2022 final rule are suspended; the prior regulatory baseline applies for newly originated loans.
  • What survives. Loans originated before the suspension's effective date remain governed by the 2022 final rule's procedures. Statutory borrower-defense rights remain.
  • Interaction with earnings-premium consequence. A program that loses Direct Loan eligibility (M05) at AY 2028-29 produces no new borrowers from that year forward. Pre-existing borrowers retain whatever BD/CSD pathway applied at their loan-origination date — the 2022 final rule's procedures for loans originated under that regime, the prior baseline for loans originated under the suspension.
  • Litigation context. The 2022 BD/CSD final rule has faced ongoing legal challenges; the OBBBA suspension does not dispose of those cases on the merits. Outcome of pending litigation could change which rule governs which origination cohort.

This panel exists for institutions whose strategic planning reaches beyond the next two measurement cycles. The BD/CSD timeline interacts with closure decisions (M11, M15) and with the wind-down window for affected borrowers.
