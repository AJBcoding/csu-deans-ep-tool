M11

Institutional administrative compliance pathway. ECAR updates, PPA renewals, § 668.14 / § 668.16 admin-capability filings, and § 668.408 reporting are the institutional-side operational obligations driven by program-level outcomes.

Each verdict the tool reports has an institutional-side administrative footprint. Eligibility loss, disclosure obligations, and admin-capability findings each thread through specific filings: the Eligibility and Certification Approval Report (ECAR), the Program Participation Agreement (PPA), and the proposed § 668.408 reporting cadence.

How it works

  • ECAR updates. A program that loses eligibility, is restored, is established, discontinued, or undergoes a CIP change must be reflected on the ECAR under proposed § 600.10(c)(3) and § 600.21(a)(11)(vi). The institution files the update; the Department reviews.
  • PPA renewals and provisional status. An institution-level admin-capability finding under proposed § 668.14(h) (M06) can move the institution to provisional PPA status. Renewal reviews fold the finding into the institution's go-forward Title IV participation.
  • Admin-capability filings under § 668.16(t). The proposed admin-capability provisions extend prior GE-rule reporting concepts to all Title IV programs at the institution. New per-CIP and per-program reporting obligations attach.
  • § 668.408 reporting cadence. The institution reports per-program and per-cohort data on the schedule the Department prescribes; this is the data feed that drives each measurement window (M12).
  • Orderly closure. Proposed § 668.603(c)(4) governs orderly closure procedures when a program loses eligibility and the institution decides not to pursue re-entry under § 668.604.

The administrative layer is where strategic responses (M15) become real obligations: a merger, a CIP change, or a credential restructure each require a corresponding ECAR + PPA filing chain. This panel surfaces the filing chain so the Dean can scope the institutional-side workload.
