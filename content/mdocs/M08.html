M08

OBBBA § 81001 federal loan caps. Statutory caps on aggregate and annual federal-loan capacity run alongside the earnings-premium regime; they bind even on programs that pass the earnings test.

OBBBA § 81001 imposes new statutory caps on federal loan capacity, separate from § 84001's earnings-premium test. A graduate program can pass the earnings-premium test (M04) and still see meaningful enrollment friction from the loan caps if its students rely on Direct Loans for tuition coverage above the caps.

How it works

  • Annual cap. § 81001 sets a per-student annual cap on federal-loan disbursements, tightening the prior § 685.203 schedule. Affected: graduate-program PLUS borrowers and high-cost professional programs.
  • Aggregate cap. § 81001 also tightens lifetime federal-loan capacity. A student returning for a graduate program after undergraduate borrowing has reduced remaining headroom.
  • Effective date. The caps are statutorily self-executing; no regulatory implementation is required for them to bind. The proposed § 668 NPRM does not amend § 81001.
  • Interaction with earnings-premium test. A program that loses Direct Loan eligibility (M05) loses access for new students entirely. A program that passes the earnings-premium test still operates within the § 81001 cap structure for its borrowing students.

Empirical context: independent analyses (Kelchen and others) have estimated that roughly 38% of current graduate-program borrowers exceed the new annual cap and roughly 18% exceed the new aggregate cap, with field-by-field variation. Programs in fields with high tuition and low household income at matriculation see the largest enrollment-pipeline effects.
