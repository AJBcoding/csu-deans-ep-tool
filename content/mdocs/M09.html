M09

Appeal scope under proposed § 668.603. Calculation-error claims only. Methodology, data quality, IRS-match accuracy, and expansion outcome are not appealable through this pathway.

The proposed appeal scope is the narrowest it has been in any prior accountability rule. A program contesting a failure determination can raise calculation errors against the published flag, but cannot challenge the underlying methodology, the IRS-match construction, or the cohort-side expansion result.

How it works

  • Permitted appeal grounds. A pure arithmetic dispute — for example, the institution can show the Department's pass/fail flag is inconsistent with the Department's own published numerator and denominator — is in scope.
  • Out of scope. Claims about the IRS-match procedure (M02), the four-step expansion outcome (M01), the benchmark choice (M03), the privacy-suppression decision, or the named-population framing (M14) are not within the appeal's scope.
  • Procedure. The institution files under § 668.603(b); the Department's determination is final for the measurement year. Filing does not stay the disclosure obligation (M07).
  • Institution-level pathway. Institution-level findings under § 668.14(h) (M06) follow a separate, broader appeal track under Subpart G; that track addresses admin-capability findings, not the per-program failure determination.

Practical effect: the appeal provides a remedy when the published numbers do not arithmetically support the published flag, and almost no remedy in any other case. A program that disagrees with how the cohort or benchmark was constructed has no procedural path to contest the construction itself.
