M13

Calendar dates that matter. First measurement: calendar 2027 (federal-data release 2026). Second: calendar 2028 (federal-data release 2027). Earliest Title IV Direct Loan eligibility loss: AY 2028-29.

The rule's timing is anchored to two federal-data release calendars. The first measurement window opens in calendar 2027; the earliest year a program can lose Direct Loan eligibility is academic year 2028-29. Disclosure obligations attach earlier.

How it works

  • Cohort definition. Each measurement year uses graduates from four years prior — calendar-2027 measurement uses the AY 2022-23 / 2023-24 graduating cohort.
  • First measurement: calendar 2027. First public release of pass / fail flags under the proposed rule. First-failure disclosure obligations (M07) attach at this release.
  • Second measurement: calendar 2028. The two-of-three-year trigger (M04) becomes operable. A program with two consecutive failures here is the earliest case of eligibility loss.
  • Earliest eligibility loss: AY 2028-29. Implementation timing means new students enrolling in academic year 2028-29 are the first cohort affected; existing borrowers are protected for prior disbursements.
  • PPA assurance: July 1, 2026. The institutional assurance under OBBBA § 84001(a)(6) — the affirmative compliance promise on the Program Participation Agreement — takes effect this date. Any institution operating after July 1, 2026 has signed up to the regime.

The proposed rule retains a partial transition for grandfathered cohorts. Programs with first-time enrollees before the effective date follow the cohort-period rules in effect at enrollment; new enrollees follow the rule from their enrollment year forward.
