M12

Programs without a published measure. The 'no measure' state is operationally distinct from passing — there is no procedural anchor for appeal under the proposed § 668.603 calc-error-only scope.

A program can be "not measured" for several distinct reasons, each with different procedural implications. NOT MEASURED is not equivalent to PASS — it produces no failure count, but it also produces no published earnings figure for the program.

How it works

The four operative reasons a program produces NOT MEASURED:

  • Cohort below 30 after the four-step expansion (M01). Reported as the suppression flag cohort_suppressed. No median is published.
  • IRS reporter count below 16 for the matched cohort (proposed § 668.404(d)). The earnings record is suppressed by federal privacy rule.
  • Benchmark population below threshold. Census data in the population required by the rule (M03) does not contain enough working adults to publish; no comparison possible.
  • Out of scope of OBBBA § 84001. Some credentials (undergraduate certificates of certain types, programs at exempt institutions before the carve-out's removal) sit outside the rule's reach. The tool reports this with out_of_scope = true.

None of these states produce a failure count. None of them are appealable: the proposed § 668.603 appeal scope is "calculation error only," and a NOT MEASURED outcome is the absence of a calculation, not a calculation error. M01's expansion logic is not appealable independently either.

The snapshot-metadata footer on every result page reports which suppression reason applied, with provenance traceable to the Department's published flag.
