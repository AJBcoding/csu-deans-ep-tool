M12

Programs without a published measure. The 'no measure' state is operationally distinct from passing — there is no procedural anchor for appeal under the proposed § 668.603 calc-error-only scope.

A program can be "not measured" for five distinct reasons. NOT MEASURED is not equivalent to PASS — it produces no failure count and no published earnings figure. Absence from the federal data is logically distinct from suppression: a missing row is not a privacy decision.

How it works

The five operative reasons:

  • Cohort below the 30-graduate floor after the four-step expansion (M01). Flag: cohort_suppressed. Source: proposed § 668.403(d).
  • Privacy-suppressed cell. The federal data carries a row-level privacy mask on the program's earnings, applied for any qualifying privacy-protection reason. The figure is withheld regardless of cohort size.
  • Out of scope of OBBBA § 84001. Some credentials (certain undergraduate certificates, programs at previously-exempt institutions) sit outside the rule's reach. Flag: out_of_scope = true.
  • Invisible to the federal data (Persona-A queried-code path). A Dean queries a specific code (for example, Cinematic Arts at CSULB, baccalaureate, code 50.0601) and the federal data returns zero rows — even though IPEDS Completions shows hundreds of graduates in the relevant award years. Absence is logically distinct from suppression: no privacy decision, no cohort finding, only a row that never appeared. Surfaced with the b16-invisibility provenance line.
  • IRS-records floor (proposed § 668.404(d)). Fewer than 16 graduates appear in the IRS-match for the measurement year. Distinct from the broader privacy-suppressed flag above; this is the proposed regulation's specific numeric trigger.

None produce a failure count. None are appealable: the proposed § 668.603 appeal scope is "calculation error only," and NOT MEASURED is the absence of a calculation. The b16-invisibility case has no procedural anchor — no published flag to contest.

The snapshot-metadata footer reports which reason applied, traceable to the Department's published flag (or to the missing row, in the b16 case).
