M04

Operational specificity. Two annual failures within three measurement years triggers Title IV Direct Loan ineligibility. Disclosure obligations attach on first failure.

The pass / fail trigger is a stateful rule, not a single-year cliff. A program fails an award year if its measured graduate-cohort median is below the benchmark for that year. The consequence escalates by failure count.

How it works

  • One failure. Disclosure obligations attach: the institution must publish the federal warning language on admissions materials and student-facing surfaces for the failing program (M07).
  • Two failures within any three consecutive measurement years. The program loses eligibility for Title IV Direct Loans for new students. Existing borrowers are unaffected for prior disbursements (M05).
  • Re-entry. A program restored to compliance must clear the published procedure under the proposed § 668.604; the institution may not restart enrollment without an affirmative determination.

"Failure" is the single-year arithmetic comparison the engine reproduces. The Department's pre-computed pass/fail flags are surfaced separately and the tool flags any disagreement between its own computation and the published flag (R20 cross-validation).

The proposed appeal scope under § 668.603 is limited to calculation-error claims. Disagreement with the methodology, the data quality of the IRS match, or the cohort-side expansion outcome is not appealable.
