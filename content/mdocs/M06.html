M06

Institution-level escalation under proposed § 668.14(h) and § 668.16(t). A program-level failure pattern can become an institution-level admin-capability finding affecting Title IV beyond Direct Loans.

The program-level consequence (M05) is the federal-loan eligibility loss for a single program. A separate institution-level pathway exists: the proposed administrative-capability provisions can convert a pattern of program-level failures into a finding that affects the institution's broader Title IV participation.

How it works

  • Trigger. Proposed § 668.14(h) creates a new admin-capability gate; proposed § 668.16(t) extends prior GE-rule admin-capability concepts to all Title IV programs at the institution. The Department reaches the institution-level layer when it determines the program-level failures reflect a broader compliance or capability concern.
  • Standard. The Department's preamble describes the gate using "AND/OR" language linking failure-pattern severity to other admin-capability indicators. The exact threshold is parametric to the Department's determination, not a numeric rule.
  • Consequence. A finding under § 668.14(h) can place the institution on provisional PPA status, with potential effects on Pell Grant, Federal Work-Study, and other Title IV programs beyond Direct Loans.
  • Appeal. Institution-level findings under § 668.14(h)(2) follow a different appeal track from program-level appeals (M09). The Subpart G enforcement framework runs in parallel.

Cross-validation banner: the engine surfaces this panel when the program's institution shows a failure pattern across multiple programs in the same measurement year. Whether the pattern triggers an actual § 668.14(h) finding is the Department's determination; the tool reports the empirical pattern, not the regulatory outcome.
