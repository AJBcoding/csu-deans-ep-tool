M05

Title IV Direct Loan ineligibility scope. Students cannot take new federal loans to enroll in an ineligible program; existing borrowers are unaffected for prior disbursements.

The program-level consequence is loss of Title IV Direct Loan availability for newly enrolling students. The scope is narrower than total Title IV ineligibility — Pell and other Title IV programs are addressed separately under the institution-level provisions (M06).

How it works

  • Affected students. New enrollees in the program after the eligibility-loss effective date cannot originate Direct Loans (Subsidized, Unsubsidized, or PLUS) for that program.
  • Continuing borrowers. Existing students with disbursed loans remain eligible to complete their program with continuing Direct Loan disbursements during the wind-down window provided by the proposed § 668.604(c).
  • Other federal aid. Pell Grant and Federal Work-Study eligibility for the program is governed by the institution-level admin-capability provisions (proposed § 668.14(h) and § 668.16(t)), not by this rule. M06 covers the institutional escalation pathway.
  • Institutional reporting. The institution must update its Eligibility and Certification Approval Report (ECAR) and PPA to reflect the loss; this is the M11 administrative-action layer.

Direct Loan ineligibility is reversible if the program returns to passing under the proposed § 668.604 re-entry procedure. The OBBBA loan-cap regime under § 81001 (M08) interacts with this consequence: students who lose access to Direct Loans simultaneously face the new statutory caps on remaining federal-loan capacity.
