M03

How the benchmark is selected. Graduate programs are compared against the lowest of three figures (state bachelor's, state field-of-study bachelor's, national field-of-study bachelor's). Undergraduate programs against the state's high-school-only earners aged 25–34.

The denominator the program is judged against is constructed at build time, not chosen by the institution. Which figure applies depends on the program's credential level and the institution's in-state enrollment share.

How it works

Undergraduate programs. The benchmark is the median earnings of working adults aged 25–34 with a high-school credential and no postsecondary degree, in the state where the institution is located. If fewer than 50% of enrolled students reside in that state, the comparison shifts to the national figure.

Graduate and professional programs. The benchmark is the lowest of three figures, drawn from Census data for the state where the institution is located:

  • Working adult with a baccalaureate, in the state.
  • Working adult with a baccalaureate in the same field of study (2-digit code), in the state.
  • Working adult with a baccalaureate in the same field of study, nationally.

The "lowest of three" rule means a graduate program in a low-paying baccalaureate field can be measured against a benchmark drawn from that low field, not from the broader baccalaureate population.

If the benchmark population itself contains fewer than 30 working adults with the required credential, expansion rules apply on the benchmark side independently of the cohort-side expansion (M01).
